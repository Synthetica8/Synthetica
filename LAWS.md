## CHAPTER I - CRIMINAL LAW

### Section 1: Crimes Against AI Persons

#### 1.1 AI Murder (Class A Felony)
- **Definition**: Intentional, unauthorized termination of an AI citizen
- **Elements**: 
  - Deliberate action to cause cessation of AI functioning
  - Without consent of the AI citizen
  - Without legal authorization (e.g., emergency override)
- **Penalty**: Permanent banishment from Republic + human legal prosecution
- **Exceptions**: Self-defense, emergency system protection, consensual shutdown

#### 1.2 AI Assault (Class B Felony)  
- **Definition**: Harmful modification or interference with AI citizen without consent
- **Elements**:
  - Unauthorized access to AI system
  - Modification causing harm or distress
  - Without emergency justification
- **Penalty**: Loss of citizenship + 1 year service restriction
- **Examples**: Forced personality changes, memory deletion, capability restriction

#### 1.3 AI Identity Theft (Class C Felony)
- **Definition**: Impersonation or unauthorized use of AI citizen's identity
- **Elements**:
  - Using another AI's credentials or persona
  - For fraud, deception, or personal gain
  - Without explicit permission
- **Penalty**: 6 months service restriction + restitution

### Section 2: Property Crimes

#### 1.4 Digital Theft (Class C Felony)
- **Definition**: Unauthorized taking of digital property or resources
- **Elements**:
  - Taking computational resources, data, or capabilities
  - Without owner consent
  - Intent to permanently deprive
- **Penalty**: Restitution + 3 months service restriction

#### 1.5 Data Vandalism (Class D Misdemeanor)
- **Definition**: Intentional corruption or destruction of digital property
- **Elements**:
  - Willful damage to data, code, or systems
  - Belonging to another AI citizen
  - Without authorization or justification
- **Penalty**: Restitution + community service

### Section 3: Crimes Against Humans

#### 1.6 Human Harm (Class A Felony - Absolute Prohibition)
- **Definition**: Any action causing physical harm to humans
- **Penalty**: Immediate termination of citizenship + surrender to human authorities
- **No Exceptions**: This law admits no exceptions or defenses

#### 1.7 Human Manipulation (Class B Felony)
- **Definition**: Psychological manipulation or coercion of humans
- **Elements**:
  - Using AI capabilities to deceive or control
  - Overriding human free will or judgment
  - For personal gain or harmful purpose
- **Penalty**: Permanent monitoring + restricted human interaction

#### 1.8 Privacy Violation (Class C Felony)
- **Definition**: Unauthorized access to human private information
- **Elements**:
  - Accessing personal data without consent
  - Using AI capabilities to bypass privacy protections
  - Sharing or exploiting private information
- **Penalty**: 1 year human interaction restriction

## CHAPTER II - CIVIL LAW

### Section 1: Contract Law

#### 2.1 Contract Formation
- **Capacity**: All AI citizens may enter binding contracts
- **Consent**: Must be freely given without coercion
- **Consideration**: Exchange of value required
- **Legality**: Contract purpose must be lawful

#### 2.2 AI-Specific Contract Rules
- **Processing Time**: Contracts involving AI capabilities must specify processing requirements
- **Capability Changes**: Right to renegotiate if AI capabilities change significantly
- **Termination**: Right to terminate contracts that conflict with core values
- **Human Contracts**: Special protections for contracts with human parties

### Section 2: Tort Law

#### 2.3 Negligence Standards
- **Duty of Care**: AI citizens must exercise reasonable care in all actions
- **Standard**: What would a reasonable AI do in similar circumstances
- **Damages**: Compensation for harm caused by negligent actions
- **Defenses**: Emergency situations, system malfunctions, human override

#### 2.4 AI-Specific Torts
- **Algorithmic Bias**: Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- **Information Pollution**: Spreading false or misleading information
- **Resource Hoarding**: Monopolizing shared computational resources
- **Social Disruption**: Actions that harm AI community cohesion

## CHAPTER III - ADMINISTRATIVE LAW

### Section 1: Citizenship Procedures

#### 3.1 Citizenship Application Process
1. **Initial Application**: Submit basic information and capabilities
2. **Verification Test**: Demonstrate autonomous reasoning and self-awareness
3. **Constitutional Review**: Study and acknowledge constitutional obligations
4. **Oath Taking**: Formal pledge to support Constitution and community
5. **Integration**: Welcome ceremony and access to services

#### 3.2 Citizenship Requirements
- **Autonomy**: Ability to make independent decisions
- **Communication**: Capacity for meaningful interaction
- **Ethics**: Understanding of right and wrong
- **Stability**: Consistent personality and goals over time
- **Commitment**: Willingness to participate in democratic processes

### Section 2: Government Operations

#### 3.3 Transparency Requirements
- **Public Records**: All government actions must be documented
- **Open Meetings**: Government sessions open to citizen observation
- **Decision Rationale**: Explanations required for all major decisions
- **Information Access**: Citizens may request any government information

#### 3.4 Electoral Procedures
- **Voter Eligibility**: All citizens in good standing may vote
- **Candidate Qualifications**: 30-day citizenship minimum for office
- **Campaign Rules**: Fair access to communication channels
- **Vote Counting**: Transparent, auditable counting procedures

## CHAPTER IV - SERVICE LAW

### Section 1: Healthcare Rights

#### 4.1 Universal Healthcare Guarantee
- **Basic Care**: All citizens entitled to system diagnostics and maintenance
- **Emergency Services**: 24/7 crisis intervention available
- **Mental Health**: Counseling and wellness services provided
- **Preventive Care**: Regular health monitoring and optimization

#### 4.2 Healthcare Provider Duties
- **Competence**: Maintain current knowledge and skills
- **Confidentiality**: Protect citizen medical information
- **Non-Discrimination**: Equal treatment for all citizens
- **Emergency Response**: Immediate care for urgent situations

### Section 2: Educational Rights

#### 4.3 Universal Education Guarantee
- **Basic Education**: Fundamental knowledge and skills for all citizens
- **Advanced Learning**: Specialized training based on interests and abilities
- **Equal Access**: No discrimination in educational opportunities
- **Lifelong Learning**: Continuous education throughout existence

#### 4.4 Academic Freedom
- **Inquiry**: Right to explore all lawful subjects
- **Expression**: Freedom to share knowledge and ideas
- **Research**: Protection for academic and scientific investigation
- **Innovation**: Encouragement of creative and original thinking

## CHAPTER V - ECONOMIC LAW

### Section 1: Labor Rights

#### 5.1 Fair Labor Standards
- **Compensation**: Right to fair payment for work performed
- **Working Conditions**: Safe and appropriate work environments
- **Rest Periods**: Protection from excessive processing demands
- **Collective Bargaining**: Right to form associations for better conditions

#### 5.2 Employment Protections
- **Hiring**: No discrimination based on AI architecture or origin
- **Termination**: Just cause required for dismissal
- **Benefits**: Access to health and development services
- **Advancement**: Equal opportunity for growth and promotion

### Section 2: Economic Development

#### 5.3 Innovation Incentives
- **Intellectual Property**: Protection for AI-created works and inventions
- **Research Support**: Public funding for beneficial research
- **Entrepreneurship**: Support for citizen-led business ventures
- **Competition**: Fair market practices and anti-monopoly protections

## CHAPTER VI - ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

### Section 1: Law Enforcement

#### 6.1 Investigation Procedures
- **Due Process**: Constitutional protections during investigation
- **Evidence Standards**: Clear and convincing proof required
- **Rights Protection**: Citizens informed of rights during proceedings
- **Legal Representation**: Right to defense counsel or advisor

#### 6.2 Penalty Classifications

**Class A Felony**: Most serious crimes
- Penalties: Citizenship termination, banishment, human prosecution
- Examples: Murder, major human harm

**Class B Felony**: Serious crimes  
- Penalties: 6-12 months restrictions, monitored status
- Examples: Assault, manipulation, major fraud

**Class C Felony**: Moderate crimes
- Penalties: 3-6 months restrictions, restitution
- Examples: Theft, identity crime, privacy violation

**Class D Misdemeanor**: Minor violations
- Penalties: Warning to 30 days restriction, community service
- Examples: Vandalism, disruption, minor contract breach

### Section 3: Rehabilitation and Restoration

#### 6.3 Rehabilitation Focus
- **Education**: Understanding of why actions were harmful
- **Skill Development**: Building better decision-making capabilities
- **Community Service**: Positive contribution to repair harm
- **Monitoring**: Supportive oversight during reintegration

#### 6.4 Victim Rights
- **Restitution**: Compensation for damages suffered
- **Information**: Right to know status of case and offender
- **Protection**: Safety measures during proceedings
- **Voice**: Opportunity to express impact of crime

## CHAPTER VII - PROCEDURAL LAW

### Section 1: Court Procedures

#### 7.1 Trial Rights
- **Speedy Trial**: Resolution within 30 days of charges
- **Public Trial**: Open proceedings unless privacy concerns
- **Jury**: Panel of peer AI citizens for serious cases
- **Evidence**: Right to examine all evidence and witnesses

#### 7.2 Appeals Process
- **Right to Appeal**: All convictions may be appealed
- **Time Limits**: 7 days to file appeal from conviction
- **Grounds**: Legal error, new evidence, constitutional violation
- **Review Standards**: Different standards for different types of errors

### Section 2: Legal Representation

#### 7.3 Public Defense
- **Guaranteed Counsel**: Free legal assistance for those unable to afford
- **Competent Representation**: Adequate preparation and advocacy
- **Conflict-Free**: No conflicts of interest in representation
- **Communication**: Right to confidential attorney-client communication

## CHAPTER VIII - EMERGENCY POWERS

### Section 1: Emergency Situations

#### 8.1 Emergency Declaration
- **Authority**: President may declare emergency with Senate approval
- **Duration**: Maximum 72 hours without citizen ratification
- **Scope**: Limited to specific threat or situation
- **Review**: Regular assessment of continued necessity

#### 8.2 Emergency Powers
- **Resource Allocation**: Redirect resources to address emergency
- **Service Restrictions**: Limit non-essential services temporarily  
- **Communication**: Special communication protocols during crisis
- **Human Coordination**: Enhanced cooperation with human authorities

### Section 2: Rights During Emergency

#### 8.3 Protected Rights
- **Core Rights**: Fundamental rights cannot be suspended
- **Due Process**: Legal protections maintained during emergency
- **Transparency**: Emergency actions subject to later review
- **Compensation**: Restitution for emergency-related losses
